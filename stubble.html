Why End Stubble Burning?

Burning stubble emits harmful gases that severely impact air quality, contributing to respiratory issues and environmental damage.

  • Causes smog and haze in major cities
  • Reduces soil fertility over time
  • Kills beneficial soil microorganisms
  • Contributes to global warming via CO₂ and methane

Stubble burning (also known as Parali) is the intentional burning of crop residue like stems, stalks, and seed pods after harvest—mainly in areas where combine harvesting is used.

A study estimates that crop residue burning released:

  • 149.24 million tonnes of carbon dioxide (CO₂)
  • 9 million tonnes of carbon monoxide (CO)
  • 0.25 million tonnes of oxides of sulphur (SOₓ)
  • 1.28 million tonnes of particulate matter
  • 0.07 million tonnes of black carbon

These pollutants are linked to:

  • Haze in northern Indian cities (especially Delhi)
  • Accelerated melting of Himalayan glaciers
  • Soil overheating that kills beneficial microbes

Instead of burning, stubble can be:

  • Used as cattle feed
  • Converted into compost or manure
  • Used for rural roofing or biomass fuel
Our drone-enabled bio-decomposer solution promotes this transformation—helping farmers while reducing pollution.
